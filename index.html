The Problem

The healthcare affordability crisis is deeper than the fact that costs are increasing at twice the rate of inflation. Industry profits are increasing even faster — four times as fast as inflation. The savings employers were promised from industry consolidation and innovation never trickled down to employers who can't track the waste hidden inside their data.

What's preventing employers from benefiting from industry efficiencies is the waste they never see. The money that leaks out of the company checking account the plan never knew it paid. These leaks come in three forms:

  1. Above-market prices the plan never saw
  2. Low-quality care the plan never revealed
  3. Declining member health the plan could not track

The salt in this wound is the patchwork of vendors addressing these three areas. They operate in silos, self-reports savings, and focus on their ROI, rather than overall plan performance. Missed opportunities, conflict with other vendors' actions, and generalized "savings" estimates never make the reports. It is as much a disservice to high-performing vendors as it is to the plan.
